Parent Letters

This section explains when parent notification letters are used, how the workflow should be handled, and what precautions a Graduate Assistant should take before a letter is prepared or sent.

Module Focus Communication

Parent letters must be handled carefully, with supervisor guidance, accurate records, and policy-based communication.

Parent Notification Workflow

Parent letters are used only when policy criteria are met and the office has determined that parent notification is appropriate. These letters should never be treated as routine mailings without confirming that the case, student status, and workflow requirements have been reviewed properly.

Request-to-Send Steps

  1. Receive the approved request from the conduct officer or supervisor.
  2. Confirm the mailing address using official student records.
  3. Draft the letter using the appropriate office template.
  4. Route the draft for supervisor edits or approval if needed.
  5. Send the final version through the approved process.
  6. Log the date and relevant workflow notes in Maxient or the appropriate office record.
Important Practice

Parent letters should be factual, policy-based, and carefully reviewed. Do not add unnecessary case detail, assumptions, or personal wording that goes beyond the approved office purpose.

Communication Cautions

  • Keep language factual and policy-based.
  • Avoid over-disclosure of case details.
  • Verify the correct student and correct address before sending anything.
  • Escalate unusual family requests or legal concerns to a supervisor.
  • Do not promise mailing changes or delivery exceptions without approval.

Before Sending a Parent Letter

Special Situations

Sometimes a student may request a different mailing address or raise concerns about family notification. These situations should not be handled casually. Gather the request, document it appropriately, and bring it to the supervisor for direction before making any change to the normal process.
