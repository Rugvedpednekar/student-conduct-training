Minimum Sanctioning Guidelines

This section is an internal-use reference for sanction selection, completion deadlines, parent notification expectations, seasonal GIPP guidance, and hold-related workflow review.

Sanction Workflow Internal Guide

The sanction start date is the date the resolution email is sent, not the date of the incident.

Core Operational Rules

  • For sanctions with a time limit, the start date is the date the resolution email is sent.
  • Always review prior sanctions before determining current sanctions.
  • OSCCR coordinates financial charges for online classes.
  • If a hearing officer wants to assign sanctions beyond the listed minimums, they should consult Dave before drafting the resolution.
  • If a student is found responsible and Maxient shows an athletic affiliation, copy Alicia Queally on the hearing outcome email.

Hold Removal Workflow

  1. Verify that completion evidence is received and valid.
  2. Confirm time-based sanctions have reached the correct end date.
  3. Update Maxient and any required tracking records.
  4. Submit for supervisor review if hold removal is needed.
  5. Supervisor approves and initiates the hold removal process.
Supervisor-Only Action

GAs do not independently remove holds, modify sanction deadlines, or assign non-standard sanctions without direct supervisor authorization.

Seasonal GIPP Rule

From August 26 to April 7, use GIPP Grad Meeting Assigned. From April 8 to August 25, do not use GIPP Grad Meeting Assigned; use the educational paper in lieu of GIPP.

Alcohol Policy

Alcohol 1st Time Violation

Applicable sections: 9A, 9B, 9C, 9D, 9F, 9I, 9J, or 9H if the student is under 21.

  • Under the Influence (3rd Millennium), due in 2 weeks; hearing officer selects “first time.”
  • August 26–April 7: assign GIPP Grad Meeting, due in 3 weeks.
  • April 8–August 25: do not assign GIPP Grad Meeting; use educational paper instead.
  • Disciplinary Probation for 3 months.
  • Parent notification after appeal window closes if student is under 21 at time of letter.
  • Copy Carlene Davidson and GIPPGrads@hartford.edu in the hearing resolution.

Alcohol 1st Time — Low-Level Sections

Applicable sections: 9E excessive amount, 9G drinking paraphernalia with no residue, and/or 9H displaying alcohol-related materials for 21+ students.

  • Disciplinary Warning.

Alcohol 2nd Time Violation

For all alcohol sections except E, G, and H.

  • Under the Influence (3rd Millennium), due in 2 weeks; hearing officer selects “second time.”
  • August 26–April 7: assign GIPP Grad Meeting, due in 3 weeks.
  • April 8–August 25: use educational paper in lieu of GIPP.
  • $120 administrative cost is already built into sanction language; do not list separately.
  • Disciplinary Probation for 12 months.
  • Parent notification after appeal window closes if student is under 21.
  • E-Checkup summer assignment; no scheduled GIPP Grad meeting.
  • Copy Carlene Davidson and GIPPGrads@hartford.edu in the hearing resolution.

Alcohol 2nd Time — Sections E / G / H

Applicable sections: E excessive amount, only G drinking paraphernalia with no residue, H displaying alcohol-related materials, or empties found in a room with 21+ occupants.

  • Under the Influence (3rd Millennium), due in 2 weeks; hearing officer selects “first time.”
  • August 26–April 7: assign GIPP Grad Meeting, due in 3 weeks.
  • April 8–August 25: use educational paper in lieu of GIPP.
  • Disciplinary Probation for 1 month.

Fire Safety / Housing / Vacancy

Failure to Keep Vacancy

  • Set a specific re-adjustment deadline and date for RD visual inspection.
  • Assign reflection paper and attach the maintain vacancy written assignment form.
  • Reflection paper due in one week.

Operational note: A first instance may be treated like a prohibited-item first-time issue with an email reminder; a second time results in a conduct hearing.

Fire Safety — 1st Time

Examples: lit candle or lit incense unattended, covered smoke detector, purposefully removed smoke detector.

  • Deferred Permanent Removal from housing.
  • Fire Safety Online Safety Course.
  • Give one week to complete the 20-minute Vector training.
  • Copy stconduct@hartford.edu and stender@hartford.edu in hearing resolution email.

Fire Safety — 2nd Time

Examples: lit candle or lit incense unattended, covered smoke detector, purposefully removed smoke detector.

  • Permanent Removal from housing.
  • Include move-out date one week from hearing resolution.
  • Include check-out instructions through Residential Life.
  • Student may be interimly suspended before move-out if further behavior is reported.

Damage to University Property

  • Fire Safety Online Safety Course.
  • Give one week to complete the 20-minute Vector training.
  • Copy stconduct@hartford.edu and stender@hartford.edu in hearing resolution email.

Prohibited Items / Repeated Behavior Rules

  • Drug/drug paraphernalia, alcohol, and lit candle/lit incense always result in a student conduct hearing.
  • All other prohibited items: no conduct meeting on first instance; send reminder email from Maxient explaining why the item is not allowed.
  • If a second occurrence happens for a prohibited item that did not have a conduct hearing the first time, the second instance results in a hearing.
  • If found responsible after that second instance, assign reflection paper or another educational assignment plus a low-level punitive sanction after consultation.
  • Two instances of an unlit candle in a room: the second instance results in a hearing, and if found responsible, the Fire Safety Online Course can be assigned.
  • Messy room or items hanging from the ceiling: RA documents internally first; if not corrected, a Maxient report is submitted and the RD sends a warning/policy reminder email via template letter 14.

Fire Safety Class / Truth and Consequences Reference

Truth and Consequences Class Can Be Used For

  • Cleanliness
  • Candles that are new / never lit, or dry wax situations, if repeated
  • Quiet hours
  • Items on ceiling, sprinkler pipes, and similar repeated issues

Fire Safety Class / Reflection Paper Can Be Used For

  • Pulling a fire alarm when there is no fire
  • Tampering with equipment, including covering smoke detectors
  • Failure to evacuate during drills
  • Lit candle, attended or unattended
  • Lit incense, attended or unattended

In some situations, the reflection paper assignment can be used in lieu of the fire safety class.
