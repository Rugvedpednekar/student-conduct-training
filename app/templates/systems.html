Office Systems

This section explains the major systems used in Student Conduct operations, why each system matters, and how a Graduate Assistant should use them accurately in daily workflow.

Module Focus Systems Training

Learn the tools first, because most daily GA tasks depend on Maxient, Outlook, and sanction tracking systems.

Maxient

Maxient is the primary case-management platform used by the Office of Student Conduct. It is where reports, case records, hearing-related information, documentation, sanction information, and other conduct workflow details are organized and reviewed.

Why We Use Maxient

  • To keep conduct records organized in one place.
  • To review incident reports and related case information.
  • To document conduct workflow accurately and consistently.
  • To support hearing preparation, outcome tracking, and case follow-up.
  • To maintain an official internal record of conduct-related activity.

How a GA Uses Maxient

  • Review whether a report or student may already have a related case.
  • Check case details before communication or follow-up.
  • Review notes, status updates, sanctions, and hearing-related workflow information.
  • Help support documentation accuracy before and after hearings.
  • Use Maxient carefully and consistently, since incomplete records can affect later workflow.
Important Maxient Practice

Before updating anything, confirm you are working on the correct student and the correct case. Always cross-check details carefully and ask for guidance if a record looks unclear, duplicated, or incomplete.

Maxient Reminders

Outlook

Outlook is used for daily communication in Student Conduct. Much of the GA workflow begins in Outlook, especially when reviewing new messages, Public Safety reports, student questions, hearing communication, and internal office follow-up.

How to Use Outlook Well

  • Check inbox messages regularly throughout the work period.
  • Read messages carefully before responding.
  • Use a professional tone in all student and staff communication.
  • Confirm details before replying to conduct-related questions.
  • Keep subject lines, attachments, and recipients accurate.
Signature Reminder

Update your Outlook signature so it reflects your own name and role correctly. Do not leave another person’s name or an old signature in your outgoing emails.

Outlook Calendar

Outlook Calendar is important for tracking hearings, scheduling meetings, and staying aware of time-sensitive workflow. It helps ensure that hearing logistics, reminders, and office timing stay organized.

How to Use Outlook Calendar

  • Review scheduled hearings and appointments regularly.
  • Confirm dates, times, and participants carefully.
  • Use calendar awareness to avoid missed deadlines or overlaps.
  • Make sure hearing timing and follow-up tasks are aligned with office workflow.

3rd Millennium

3rd Millennium is used for educational sanctions such as online courses and related sanction completion tracking. It helps the office verify whether a student has completed an assigned educational requirement.

What It Is Used For

  • Checking completion of sanctions like Under the Influence or Marijuana 101.
  • Confirming whether a student has completed an assigned online educational module.
  • Supporting sanction follow-up and overdue review workflow.

How to Access It

Access should be provided through the office’s approved process and account setup. Use the official office login or approved access method provided during training. If access is missing or something does not look correct, ask for help instead of trying to work around it.

How to Check Whether a Student Completed a Sanction

  • Primary method: search using the student’s ID.
  • Also usable: search using the student’s email address.
  • Use name only if needed: search by student name only when the name is unique enough to avoid confusion.
Verification Reminder

Do not mark a sanction as complete unless it has been properly verified. If completion is not appearing clearly, double-check the search method and confirm the record carefully before updating anything.
