Office Overview

This section introduces the purpose of the Office of Student Conduct, the Graduate Assistant role, and the campus partnerships that support daily casework, student communication, and conduct operations.

Mission of Student Conduct

The Office of Student Conduct supports student growth, accountability, and community standards through fair, educational, and consistent conduct processes. The office helps students understand expectations, respond to policy concerns, and move through the conduct process in a way that is organized, respectful, and development-focused.

FERPA & Confidentiality Reminder

Student information must only be used for legitimate office purposes. Do not discuss identifiable case details outside approved workflows or with anyone who is not authorized to receive that information.

What the GA Role Supports

  • Monitoring office communications and supporting case-related follow-up.
  • Helping with hearing scheduling, notices, reminders, and documentation workflow.
  • Supporting sanctions tracking, parent-letter workflow, and internal case organization.
  • Maintaining professionalism, accuracy, and supervisor-guided communication.
  • Identifying when something needs clarification and bringing it to Dave before moving forward.

Professional Expectations

A Graduate Assistant in Student Conduct is expected to communicate professionally, keep records organized, protect confidential information, and follow office process carefully. Much of the role involves noticing details, maintaining consistency, and asking good questions before taking action.
