GA Responsibilities

This page outlines the recurring responsibilities of a Graduate Assistant in Student Conduct, including daily workflow expectations, weekly operational tasks, and habits that help maintain accurate, timely, and professional office support.

Module Focus Operations

Strong daily and weekly habits help keep hearings, sanctions, communications, and case follow-up on track.

Daily Responsibilities

  • Review the Student Conduct inbox and monitor new messages carefully.
  • Check for new incident reports, hearing-related communication, and follow-up needs.
  • Review Maxient updates and confirm whether open cases need notes, reminders, or status updates.
  • Compare relevant Public Safety reports with existing conduct records when needed.
  • Confirm upcoming hearings, deadlines, and time-sensitive student communication.
  • Flag unclear situations, duplicate case concerns, or anything needing supervisor review.
  • Maintain professional and FERPA-safe communication throughout the day.

Weekly Responsibilities

  • Review overdue sanctions and help track missing completion items.
  • Support parent-letter workflow when policy criteria are met.
  • Review sanction-related fees, charges, or completion tracking items as assigned.
  • Help with hold-related review workflow when documentation is ready for supervisor review.
  • Organize recurring follow-up items that need to be closed or escalated.
  • Support Student Conduct awareness efforts, including office communication or outreach tasks if assigned.

Morning Workflow Checklist

As-Needed Responsibilities

  • Support hearing preparation and hearing logistics.
  • Draft or prepare follow-up communication for review.
  • Assist with sanctions verification and completion checks.
  • Help route parent letters and related records appropriately.
  • Respond to unusual workflow needs based on supervisor direction.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Missing same-day case notes or follow-up steps after hearings.
  • Not cross-checking student identifiers before updating a case.
  • Assuming a sanction is complete without proper verification.
  • Sending communication before confirming details or approval needs.
  • Moving forward on unclear issues instead of asking for guidance.

Suggested Work Rhythm

Start with time-sensitive communication and urgent workflow items first. Then move into documentation, case review, and tracking tasks. End the day by checking for unresolved follow-up, confirming hearing preparation status, and making sure open items are clearly organized for the next work period.

Important Practice

Daily consistency matters more than speed. A GA should aim to be organized, accurate, professional, and proactive about asking questions before something becomes a larger issue.
