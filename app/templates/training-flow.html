GA Training Flow

This guided learning path is designed to help a Graduate Assistant understand the office step by step, build confidence with systems and workflow, and demonstrate readiness through a knowledge check at the end.

Training Structure 7 Learning Phases

Complete the phases in order so that each topic builds on the last one, just like a structured online course.

Course Overview

This training flow is organized to help a new GA move from general office understanding to practical workflow support. Each phase focuses on a part of the job that is important for daily success in Student Conduct.

1

Office Orientation

Understand the purpose of Student Conduct, the role of the office, and the expectations of a GA.

2

Systems Training

Learn how Maxient, Outlook, Outlook Calendar, and 3rd Millennium support casework and communication.

3

Daily and Weekly Workflow

Learn the recurring responsibilities that help the office stay organized and responsive.

4

Case Handling

Understand how reports are reviewed, hearings are scheduled, materials are prepared, and follow-up is completed.

5

Sanctions and Tracking

Review sanctioning guidelines, completion verification, and supervisor-controlled actions such as hold removal.

6

Communication and Parent Letter Workflow

Use the correct tone, review templates carefully, and handle parent notification workflow appropriately.

7

Readiness Check

Confirm understanding through a short quiz that checks systems knowledge, workflow judgment, and office expectations.

How to Use This Training

Goal

The goal is not only to finish the modules, but to understand the office well enough to support daily work confidently and responsibly.

Recommended Learning Sequence

1. Office Overview

Start with mission, office role, collaborations, and confidentiality expectations.

2. Systems

Learn how Maxient, Outlook, Outlook Calendar, and 3rd Millennium are used.

3. Responsibilities

Understand daily, weekly, and as-needed workflow expectations.

4. Case Handling

Review the case lifecycle from report intake to closure.

5. Sanctions

Study sanction rules, completion checks, and hold-related limitations.

6. Parent Letters

Understand parent notification workflow, address checks, and communication caution points.

7. Templates

Review what template to use, when to use it, and how to adapt it carefully.

Knowledge Check

Answer these questions after reviewing the training modules. This section is designed like a simple course checkpoint.

1. What is the primary system used for conduct-related case records and documentation?

2. What should a GA do if a case detail is unclear or a record may be duplicated?

3. What is the primary method for checking sanction completion in 3rd Millennium?

4. Can a GA independently remove a hold or assign non-standard sanctions?

5. What tone should be used in Student Conduct communication?

Completion Goal

By the end of this training flow, a GA should understand the office structure, systems, major workflows, communication expectations, and when to ask for guidance instead of guessing.
